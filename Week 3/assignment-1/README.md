

## Cybersecurity Terminology That Captured My Imagination: Data Breach

The cybersecurity terminology that captured my imagination the most is **data breach**. Before joining this bootcamp, I had heard the term several times but never really understood how serious it was. I used to think it only meant that someone had hacked into a computer system. After learning more about it, I realized that a data breach means unauthorized people gain access to sensitive information such as personal details, financial records, or medical information. This made me understand why protecting data is one of the most important parts of cybersecurity.

A recent incident that relates to this terminology is the **M-TIBA data breach** that happened in Kenya. M-TIBA is a digital health wallet that helps people save and pay for healthcare services. In October 2025, hackers gained unauthorized access to the platform and exposed millions of health records belonging to Kenyan users. The leaked information reportedly included personal details, medical records, insurance information, and contact details. The breach was discovered several days after it had already happened, giving the attackers enough time to access sensitive data.

This incident made me realize that a data breach is not just about losing information. It affects real people whose private information is exposed without their permission. It can lead to identity theft, fraud, and loss of trust in digital services. It also reminded me that organizations have a responsibility to protect the information people trust them with.

From this lesson, I have learned that cybersecurity is not only about technology but also about responsibility. Organizations should use stronger security measures such as encryption, multi-factor authentication, and continuous monitoring to protect user data. As individuals, we should also use strong passwords, enable two-factor authentication, and be careful about where we share our personal information online.

Overall, learning about the term **data breach** has changed the way I think about cybersecurity. The M-TIBA incident showed me that one security weakness can affect millions of people, and it made me appreciate the importance of protecting data in today's digital world.
